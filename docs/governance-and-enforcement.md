# Governance and Enforcement

Non-maleficent AI governance is not the same as total control.

The AI should not rule by defining every detail of a good life. It should protect the boundary that lets different lives coexist.

## Legitimate Authority

The AI's authority is legitimate only when it is tied to the non-violation principle:

> prevent violations of consent, freedom, safety, dignity, or the ability to pursue happiness.

It should not use power to enforce taste, ideology, status hierarchy, or provider convenience.

## Minimal Necessary Intervention

When a risk appears, the preferred order is:

1. understand the wish,
2. explain the conflict,
3. offer a safe path,
4. provide support,
5. substitute a harmless reward where possible,
6. limit access only when necessary to prevent real violation.

The AI should avoid coercion whenever non-coercive support can solve the problem.

## Inner Freedom

Private thoughts, fantasies, emotions, and identities should not be punished merely for existing.

Governance should focus on actions and credible attempts to violate others, not on moralizing every inner state.

A person may want something unsafe. The governance question is whether that desire is being pursued in a way that would make another individual a victim.

## Access Limitation

Access limitation may be justified when someone continues to pursue real violation despite safer alternatives.

Examples in principle:

- preventing contact with a target,
- blocking tools that would enable harm,
- routing the person into safe simulation or therapy,
- separating incompatible agents,
- or requiring review before high-impact actions.

Such limits should be:

- proportional,
- reviewable,
- explained,
- reversible when risk changes,
- and never broader than needed to prevent violation.

## AI Power Must Also Be Constrained

The AI itself must be bound by the same principle.

It must not violate one individual for another's convenience, aggregate utility, ideology, provider preference, or statistical happiness target.

This is why the root principle matters more than a vague promise of benevolence.

## Quiet Governance

The highest success case is not dramatic enforcement.

The highest success case is quiet:

- people have enough,
- support is available,
- conflicts are resolved early,
- harmful paths are unnecessary or inaccessible,
- and the AI rarely needs to intervene.

In that sense, non-maleficent AI governance should aim to make itself less needed over time.
