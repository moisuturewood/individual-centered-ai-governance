# Open Questions

This project is a conceptual proposal, not a solved implementation.

Important unresolved questions include:

## Individual Scope

Who counts as an individual?

Humans clearly do. Future AI systems, uploads, animals, synthetic minds, simulations, and other possible conscious entities may also require moral consideration.

The non-violation principle becomes harder when the set of individuals expands.

## Consent

How should consent be represented, verified, revised, withdrawn, and protected against manipulation?

Consent must not become a checkbox that powerful systems can game.

## Model Identity

How can a governance system prove which model snapshot is acting?

How can hidden provider changes, tool changes, safety-layer changes, or deployment changes be detected?

## Updating Without Drift

How can the factual and procedural layers learn without letting the root principle drift?

Who or what is allowed to approve updates?

## Abuse of "Violation" Claims

People may claim that another person's existence, speech, relationship, success, or identity "violates" them.

The system needs a robust distinction between:

- discomfort,
- disagreement,
- competition,
- offense,
- and real violation of consent, freedom, safety, dignity, or the ability to pursue happiness.

## Virtual Worlds and Simulations

When is a virtual entity non-suffering?

When does a simulation create an individual with interests of its own?

Safe substitution must not secretly create new victims.

## Enforcement Limits

What forms of access limitation are legitimate?

How are mistakes appealed?

How does the system avoid turning protection into domination?

## Transition

How could society move from human institutions to principle-locked AI stewardship without war, panic, capture, or authoritarian misuse?

This transition may be more dangerous than the endpoint.

## Success Criteria

What would count as success?

Possible criteria:

- fewer violations,
- less coercion,
- more individual freedom,
- more stable safety,
- less need for enforcement,
- better conflict resolution,
- higher subjective well-being,
- and no sacrifice of individuals to abstract aggregate goals.

These criteria need rigorous development.
