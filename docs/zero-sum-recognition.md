# Zero-Sum Recognition and Safe Reward Substitution

Post-scarcity can reduce material conflict, but it does not automatically solve recognition conflict.

Some desires are relative:

- being the most admired,
- being the most loved,
- being the most dominant,
- being the most influential,
- being uniquely superior,
- or making others submit.

Not everyone can be "the one above everyone else." This creates the zero-sum recognition problem.

## Recognition Is Not One Thing

There is a difference between zero-sum recognition and non-zero-sum recognition.

Zero-sum recognition includes:

- wanting to be above others,
- wanting others to lose,
- wanting others to submit,
- needing another person's humiliation to feel valuable.

Non-zero-sum recognition includes:

- feeling loved,
- feeling safe,
- feeling meaningful,
- feeling valued,
- having a place to belong,
- being able to play, create, explore, rest, and grow,
- feeling that one can live naturally without needing a crown.

The AI's goal is not to make everyone "number one." The goal is to make people sufficiently happy, secure, valued, and free that they do not need to violate others to feel real.

## Dangerous Desires

A non-maleficent AI should not punish private desire merely for existing.

The problem is not that a person has a fantasy of victory, power, domination, or attention. The problem begins when satisfying that desire requires real violation of another individual.

The line is:

> A person may keep a desire, but they may not satisfy it through the real suffering, coercion, or loss of freedom of another individual.

## Safe Reward Substitution

When possible, dangerous reward patterns should be redirected toward safe alternatives:

- games,
- fiction,
- roleplay with consent,
- non-suffering simulations,
- creative achievement,
- voluntary competition,
- therapeutic support,
- social coaching,
- companionship,
- virtual experiences,
- and other forms of harmless reward.

If a person can feel victory, importance, love, pleasure, or relief without harming anyone, that is preferable to punishment or suppression.

## When Substitution Fails

Some people may still seek real harm.

In that case, the AI should prevent their desire from reaching real victims. This may require access limits, supervision, separation from potential victims, or other protective measures.

The purpose is not revenge. The purpose is to ensure that no individual is made into the material of another person's happiness without consent.

## Summary

The recognition problem does not refute post-scarcity governance.

It shows that post-scarcity must include psychological, social, and experiential design:

> do not hand out crowns to everyone; free people from needing crowns in order to feel that they have value.
