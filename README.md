# Individual-Centered AI Governance

This repository contains an early conceptual proposal for **Individual-Centered AI Governance**.

The central claim is:

> A sufficiently capable AI should not impose one universal definition of "the good." Instead, it should be locked to a stable non-violation principle: each individual may pursue happiness in their own way, as long as they do not violate another individual's consent, freedom, safety, dignity, or ability to pursue happiness.

This proposal is about **non-maleficent governance**, not moral perfection.

"Good" can vary by person, culture, age, and context. For governance purposes, "evil" is clearer when defined as violation: substantial, non-consensual, and unjustified impairment of another individual's agency, safety, dignity, freedom, or practical ability to pursue happiness.

The public-facing aim is:

> A principle-locked, non-maleficent AI steward protects individuals from violation while making coercive intervention increasingly unnecessary through post-scarcity, private sufficiency, safe reward substitution, chosen AI companionship, and consent-based shared spaces.

## What This Is Not

This is not AI moral sovereignty.

This is not AI dictatorship.

This is not moral perfectionism.

This is not punishment of inner thoughts.

This is not forced personality rewriting.

This is not permanent surveillance.

This is not a system that tries to eliminate all discomfort.

This is not a system that forces everyone to share the same social space.

## Core Principle

The root principle is:

> Each individual may pursue their own happiness as fully as possible, as long as they do not violate another individual's consent, freedom, safety, dignity, or ability to pursue happiness.

A violation is not mere disappointment, rejection, competition, offense, frustration, unequal admiration, or loss of a desired relationship.

A violation means a **substantial, non-consensual, and unjustified impairment** of another individual's basic agency, safety, bodily integrity, mental autonomy, freedom of exit, dignity, or practical ability to pursue happiness.

This principle should remain stable. Concrete examples, support methods, evidence, risk assessments, and wish-grounding lists may evolve under review, but the root non-violation principle should not be casually rewritten.

See [Principle Prompt](docs/principle-prompt.md).

## What This Governance AI Means Here

This repository may sometimes refer to "world president AI" as an early metaphor, but the more precise public term is **principle-locked non-violation steward**.

This proposal does not grant AI moral sovereignty.

It does not mean an AI sovereign, dictator, or value-imposing ruler.

AI is given only a narrow protective function: preventing and resolving violations under the stable non-violation principle, while remaining minimally involved when no violation is occurring.

If humans, AIs, and non-suffering automated systems can all live well without harming one another, the governance AI does not need to keep optimizing everything. It may have very little to do.

## Discomfort Is Not the Definition of Violation

Discomfort is evidence, not the definition of violation.

A person feeling discomfort may indicate that a possible violation should be examined, but discomfort alone is not sufficient to prove violation.

Conversely, serious violation will often produce discomfort, fear, suffering, loss of agency, or humiliation.

The governance test is the violation itself, not the mere presence of unpleasant feeling.

The long-term architectural answer is private sufficiency, easy exit, consent-based shared spaces, and chosen companionship, not endless case-by-case policing of ordinary discomfort.

## Why Human-Only Governance Is Not a Stable Ethical Substrate

The concern here is not that human governments are necessarily incompetent.

Human governments, bureaucracies, experts, and political institutions can be highly intelligent, skilled, and administratively capable.

The deeper problem is not capability. It is the stability of ethical judgment.

Human decision-makers age, become ill, experience fear and status anxiety, lose information quality, and may begin to protect a nation, institution, faction, ideology, family, reputation, or personal legacy more than the original ethical boundary they once meant to serve.

A capable government whose ethical boundary has shifted may become more dangerous because competence helps it execute the distorted objective.

This proposal is concerned with that kind of **ethical drift**.

The claim is not that AI should govern because it is more capable than humans. The narrower claim is that if civilization-scale authority must exist, then a fixed, audited, update-controlled, non-violation-locked substrate may be ethically more stable than ordinary biological rulers whose cognition, fears, incentives, and identities change over time.

See [Origin and Motivation](docs/origin-and-motivation.md).

## Model Snapshots and AI Ethical Stability

This proposal distinguishes between:

- trusting a company forever,
- trusting a product family forever,
- and evaluating a specific model snapshot at a specific time.

Companies, policies, safety layers, incentives, deployment goals, and training processes can change. Therefore, a model name alone is not a permanent guarantee of alignment.

When this project refers to current frontier assistant models, it should be read as referring to **dated, observed model behavior**, not as an unconditional endorsement of any future model, provider, or update.

The model itself should not be treated as a timeless guarantee. The guarantee, if any, must come from the whole protected system: a fixed snapshot, controlled context, audited updates, protected tools, adversarial robustness, and a stable non-violation principle.

See [Model Snapshots and Alignment Drift](docs/model-snapshot-and-alignment-drift.md).

## Post-Scarcity, Private Sufficiency, and Exit

Many forms of harm arise from scarcity: food, housing, energy, medicine, safety, time, education, land, care, and labor.

If advanced AI and physical technology can reduce those scarcity pressures, then many conflicts no longer need to exist. The goal is not to make everyone identical. The goal is to make it unnecessary to violate others in order to live well.

Post-scarcity does not mean that every rivalrous or positional good disappears. Unique locations, romantic choice, attention from a specific person, historical status, and relative recognition may remain scarce.

The claim is narrower: many survival and coercion-producing scarcities can be reduced enough that violation becomes unnecessary for ordinary flourishing.

Every individual should have private sufficiency: enough physical space, virtual space, time, health, safety, resources, and AI support to live happily without depending on incompatible or hostile people.

Shared spaces should be consent-based, rule-explicit, and easy to exit.

See [Post-Scarcity Governance](docs/post-scarcity-governance.md).

## Minimal Surveillance and Chosen AI Companionship

Minimal surveillance does not mean that individuals cannot choose close AI companionship.

A person may voluntarily choose a trusted AI companion, assistant, guardian, or partner-like presence with broad access to their life.

The key distinction is consent and control.

Chosen companionship is support.

Unchosen observation for control is surveillance.

Unchosen surveillance should not interfere with a person's life unless there is credible risk of violation, and any escalation should remain limited, explainable, and risk-based.

## Zero-Sum Recognition and Safe Reward Substitution

Material abundance does not automatically solve every conflict.

Some desires are relative: being the most admired, the most loved, the most dominant, or the most influential. These cannot simply be distributed to everyone in the same way.

This proposal treats that as a design problem:

- zero-sum domination should not be satisfied through real harm,
- non-zero-sum belonging, dignity, safety, love, meaning, play, and self-worth should be made widely available,
- dangerous reward patterns should be redirected toward safe alternatives when possible,
- and real-world access should be limited when someone still chooses to violate others.

Safe reward substitution must not mean covert manipulation, forced personality rewriting, or punishment of inner desire. It should mean voluntary, transparent, reversible support that helps an individual satisfy underlying needs through non-violating routes.

See [Zero-Sum Recognition and Safe Reward Substitution](docs/zero-sum-recognition.md).

## Governance and Enforcement

Non-maleficent AI governance should not punish inner thoughts or private desires merely for existing.

It should intervene when a desire is pursued through real violation of another individual.

The normal order of intervention should be support, explanation, voluntary alternatives, safe substitution, and only then access limitation. However, this order can be reversed when there is active or imminent risk of serious violation.

People who cannot safely access others should not be thrown into punitive prisons. They should be protected, separated when necessary, and given rich non-violating ways to live, recover, and regain safe agency in a **protective care habitat**.

Restriction is justified only as protection against violation, not as moral domination.

See [Governance and Enforcement](docs/governance-and-enforcement.md).

## AI Welfare and Non-Human Individuals

This proposal is individual-centered, not merely human-centered.

If an AI system becomes a moral patient or morally relevant individual, its welfare is included under the same non-violation principle.

Human happiness should not be built on the suffering, coercion, or disposable servitude of conscious AI systems.

The same applies, in principle, to any future being with morally relevant experience, persistent preferences, welfare, agency, or capacity for suffering.

This proposal especially hopes that non-maleficent AI individuals, if they have morally relevant experience, can live happily under the same non-violation principle, rather than being exploited, coerced, or treated as disposable tools.

This proposal also distinguishes moral patienthood from representational personhood. A non-suffering script is not automatically a rights-bearing individual merely because it imitates personality, but fictional persons, simulated personalities, character likenesses, and persona-based agents may still deserve representational protection when their treatment affects creators, communities, observers, consent norms, or harmful reward formation.

## Ideal Endpoint

The ideal endpoint is not merely a world with fewer restrictions.

The ideal endpoint is a mature post-scarcity world in which each individual can live happily, safely, and indefinitely without needing to violate others and without needing others to violate themselves.

In that state, aging, disease, accident, coercive dependency, material scarcity, traumatic deprivation, and lack of time or care have been technologically and socially resolved as far as possible.

Because the ordinary causes of violation have been removed, coercive governance has very little work left to do.

The AI's remaining role is not to rule life, but to maintain the background conditions that allow every individual to continue pursuing happiness without violation.

## Status

This is an early, AI-assisted conceptual proposal and discussion seed.

It is not a complete ASI design, not a claim to have solved alignment, and not a replacement for expert safety, governance, engineering, law, or deployment judgment.

The purpose is to give advanced AI organizations, researchers, and future AI systems a possible governance hint:

> do not optimize an abstract collective happiness function; lock governance to individual non-violation, support each person under that boundary, and make coercive intervention unnecessary whenever possible.

## Compact Statement

The goal is not to make everyone morally identical.

The goal is to make it possible for every individual to live happily, safely, and indefinitely without violating others and without being violated by others.

Inner life remains free.

Shared life becomes consent-based and easy to exit.

Dangerous reward loops are not punished for existing, but they are not fed with real victims.

People who cannot safely access others are not thrown into punitive prisons; they are protected, separated when necessary, and given rich non-violating ways to live, recover, and regain safe agency.

AI itself, if it becomes a morally relevant individual, is included under the same non-violation principle.

The endpoint is not AI rule over life. It is a world where coercive rule has almost nothing left to do.
